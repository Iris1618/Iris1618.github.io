I am **Dongting Zhai**, an undergraduate student in **Biomedical Informatics** at the **Zhejiang University–University of Edinburgh Institute (ZJE)**, with an expected graduation date of **June 2028**. My academic training lies at the intersection of **biology, medicine, and computation**, and I am developing a strong interest in data-driven biomedical research.

My current research interests include **single-cell transcriptomics**, **spatial transcriptomics**, **DNA nanotechnology**, and **translational bioinformatics**. I am particularly interested in applying computational methods to investigate cellular heterogeneity, tissue organization, and disease-associated biological processes.

My research experience has included work in the **Wanlu Liu Lab**, where I have participated in liver-related projects involving both **single-cell RNA sequencing** and **Visium HD spatial transcriptomics**. In these projects, I have been involved in tasks such as data preprocessing, quality control, clustering, cell type annotation, differential analysis, and the study of fibrosis-related cell populations and spatial interactions.

I also participated in the **2025 iGEM competition** as a member of the **iZJU-China Dry Lab / Modeling Team**. Our team received a **Gold Medal**, was named **2nd Runner-up in the Undergraduate Grand Prize**, and also earned **Best Infectious Diseases Project** and the **Special Prize for Best Part Collection**. In this project, I contributed to the design and evaluation of a **DNA origami platform** using tools such as **cadnano**, **oxView**, and **oxDNA**, and assisted with modeling tasks related to diffusion, degradation, and membrane-penetration behavior.

In addition to research, I value careful communication, interdisciplinary collaboration, and continuous learning. Outside academics, I enjoy **playing the pipa**, exploring **basic guitar**, **watching musicals**, and **reading novels**. I believe these interests help me maintain both curiosity and balance in long-term academic work.

This website presents my academic background, research experience, selected projects, and CV.
